Study of the Magnetic and Structural Properties of Powders of Tb0.257-xNdxFe0.743 Mechanically Alloyed

Y A Rojas

Resumen


In this study we report the structural, magnetic and magneto-optical properties of binary and ternary amorphous rare-earth transition metal alloys of compositions Tb0.257-x NdxFe0.743 with x=0, 0.125, 0.257 prepared by mechanical alloying. The samples were milled for 48 hours in an Ar atmosphere. The saturation magnetization was measured by using a PPMS and a SQUID and investigated as a function of composition and temperature. The measurements of the hysteresis loop show that these are semi-hard magnetic compounds. The Curie and the compensation temperatures were determined. From the temperature-dependent data the compositional variation of Ms can be deduced for T= 4.2K, 77K and 300K for Nd-Fe alloys. Experimental data were also obtained by using Mössbauer 57Fe spectrometry and X-Ray Diffraction (XRD) for the x=0 and 0.128 samples. The Mössbauer spectra were obtained at room temperature and at 77K without and with a external field of 0.04T. The spectra were fitted with a sextet, a hyperfine magnetic field distribution (HMFD) and two doublets by using the program MOSFIT. The XRD analyses were made at room temperature. Patterns were obtained with a Philips difractometer with the Cu-Kα radiation and were refined by using the MAUD program. The XRD analysis identified a disordered phase based on a-Fe that presents three sites: one ferromagnetic phase associated with the HMFD, and two paramagnetic doublets.
